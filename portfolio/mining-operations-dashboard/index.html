Mining Operations
Dashboard

A two-page Power BI dashboard for a multi-site mining operation, covering production performance and safety reporting across Pilbara, Bowen Basin and Olympic Dam.

Production — Arafura Resources Operations Dashboard
The Brief

This project simulates an analytics environment for a multi-site mining operation, with fictional company Arafura Resources running production across three sites: Pilbara, Bowen Basin and Olympic Dam. Mining operations generate a significant volume of data across production, equipment, logistics and safety, and it typically lives across separate systems with no unified view.

The goal was to consolidate that into a two-page report for operational and safety teams. The production page covers output performance, equipment utilisation and cycle times by site and shift. The safety page covers injury frequency rates, near miss reporting, safety observations and incident classification, all filterable by site, shift and department.

The Approach

The report is built on a star schema data model in Power BI, with separate fact and dimension tables for production and safety data. All calculated metrics are handled in DAX, including rolling averages, variance to target, shift splits and frequency rate calculations, and are organised into measure folders to keep the model maintainable.

Safety — Arafura Resources Operations Dashboard

Separating production and safety into dedicated pages was deliberate. A production manager wants to know why Pilbara is running above cycle time target; a safety lead wants to see where near miss frequency is trending and whether observation completion is on track. Shared filters for site, shift and date range carry across both pages, so users can set their context once and move between the two views without losing it.

Key Features
Production KPIs
Tonnes mined, equipment utilisation, drill metres and average cycle time, each with variance against target.
Site-level variance table
Total tonnes mined vs target with percentage variance across Pilbara, Bowen Basin and Olympic Dam.
Equipment utilisation breakdown
Utilisation rate by equipment type: haul trucks, excavators, drill rigs, graders and dozers.
LTIFR & TRIFR tracking
Frequency rates with quarter-on-quarter movement and a 12-month LTIFR rolling trend line.
Near miss & incident log
Near misses by category, QTD incident counts by type, and a leading vs lagging severity ratio.
Safety observations vs target
Completion rate by site with colour-coded performance and days since last LTI per site.
The Outcome

The finished report covers two of the most operationally critical areas in mining: production throughput and workforce safety. Each page works independently, a production manager can go straight to their site data, while the shared filter logic means the full report reads as a single connected view.

The safety page required careful thought around metric selection. LTIFR and TRIFR are standard across the industry but they are lagging indicators by nature, so the report gives equal weight to leading measures like observation completion rates and near miss reporting. The severity ratio bar at the bottom makes that split explicit and surfaces something worth talking about in a safety review.

Mining felt like a strong sector to build for given the volume and variety of operational data involved. The principles applied here, a clear KPI hierarchy, consistent cross-page filters, a model built to extend, carry directly across to the kind of environments I have worked in and am targeting in Australia.
