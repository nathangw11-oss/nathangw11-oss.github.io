Project Write-up

Commercial Performance Dashboard

An end-to-end commercial performance dashboard tracking revenue, profit, units sold and average transaction value across e-commerce channels. Built with a comparative framework allowing users to benchmark current performance against forecast and prior year, with filters for region, sales channel and product category.

Overview — Commercial Performance Dashboard
The Brief

This project was built to simulate a commercial reporting environment for a multi-channel retail business operating across different regions and sales channels. Many reporting environments become difficult to work with because data is spread across disconnected spreadsheets or dashboards become overloaded with unnecessary detail. The goal was to bring trading, financial and operational reporting into one place, making performance easier to monitor and act on without relying on static reports or manual data pulls.

The Approach

The dashboard was built in Power BI using a star schema data model with separate fact and dimension tables. This structure improves query performance and keeps relationships between tables easier to manage as the model grows. It was intentionally kept structured and clearly labelled so that new metrics, pages or data sources could be added without needing to rebuild the report from scratch.

Star schema data model

DAX measures were used throughout for all key KPIs including revenue, profit margin, average transaction value and forecast variance, and organised into dedicated folders to keep the model clean and maintainable.

A lot of thought went into keeping the dashboard clean and easy to navigate. The aim was to make trends and performance issues easy to spot without overwhelming the user with too much information at once. I think dashboard design is part of the analytical process itself. Reporting should not only surface insight but present it in a way that people actually want to engage with.

Key Features

Executive KPI reporting with forecast vs actual analysis across all channels and regions.

A waterfall chart breaking down forecast variance by sales channel. In this example, Website (£6,403) and Amazon (£2,903) are driving the largest gaps against forecast.

Channel and regional performance tracking across EMEA and AMER.

Interactive filtering by date, channel, region, category and comparative view.

Dedicated report pages for forecasting and modelling, promotions analysis, and SKU and category performance.

Star schema data model with organised DAX measures for maintainability and efficiency.

The Outcome

The dashboard brings the full commercial picture into one place, covering everything from executive KPI summaries through to channel-level variance and promotional performance. Potential use cases include weekly trading reviews, forecasting sessions, promotional analysis and month-end reporting across channels and regions.

The approach taken here mirrors the one I applied in my role at Manchester Airports Group, where a similar focus on design quality and usability contributed to my reports ranking within the top 10 most-viewed dashboards out of roughly 8,000 reports across the business.
