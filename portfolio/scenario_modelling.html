Scenario & Sensitivity Modelling
An interactive Power BI scenario and sensitivity modelling solution designed to simulate the commercial impact of changes in traffic, conversion, average transaction value and margin across a multi-channel eCommerce environment.
The Brief

This project was built to explore how Power BI could be used beyond traditional reporting and applied within a commercial forecasting and planning environment. Most budgeting and forecasting processes still rely heavily on Excel models, and the aim here was to recreate some of that planning functionality inside a more interactive and visually accessible solution.

Instead of manually adjusting assumptions across spreadsheets, users can model commercial scenarios in real time and instantly see the impact on revenue, profit and trading performance. The project is centred around the core commercial drivers commonly used within eCommerce forecasting models: sessions, conversion rate, average transaction value, orders, revenue and profit.

The Approach

Interactive sliders allow users to adjust assumptions across session growth, conversion rate uplift, average transaction value growth and margin impact. As each assumption changes, the forecast model recalculates automatically across the full reporting layer including KPIs, cumulative revenue forecasts, revenue bridge analysis and channel-level uplift reporting.

This exposes the underlying drivers influencing commercial performance and makes scenario testing genuinely accessible for senior stakeholders who want to understand the numbers without needing to open a spreadsheet.

In the example above, session growth was reduced to -6% while conversion rate, average transaction value and margin growth were all increased. The rest of the visuals recalculated instantly to reflect the new scenario. The waterfall chart highlights the net impact to the forecast as each assumption shifts, making it straightforward to see which drivers are helping performance and which are holding it back.

Key Features
Interactive assumption sliders covering session growth, conversion rate, average transaction value and margin impact, with all visuals recalculating in real time.
Cumulative revenue forecast and waterfall bridge analysis showing the impact of each assumption change on the overall forecast.
Channel-level uplift reporting to understand how performance shifts vary across different parts of the business.
Sensitivity analysis capability, allowing users to identify which assumptions matter most for driving revenue.
SWITCH measures for dynamic KPI and metric selection, keeping the report flexible without adding visual clutter.
Disconnected tables used to support dynamic visuals and metric-driven reporting behaviour across the dashboard.
Star schema data model with clearly structured fact and dimension tables, built for maintainability and scalability.
The Outcome

The focus of the project was improving the accessibility of commercial forecasting and planning within a single reporting environment. Rather than maintaining multiple spreadsheet models and manually consolidating outputs, stakeholders can test assumptions quickly, understand the drivers behind performance changes and support faster commercial decision-making all in one place.

Potential use cases include forecast planning, budget modelling, trading reviews, promotional impact analysis, revenue sensitivity analysis and ongoing commercial performance monitoring.
