Project Write-up

Three Statement Forecast Model

A fully integrated financial model built for a fictional multi-channel outdoor retailer, covering five years of historical results and a five-year forecast across the income statement, balance sheet and cash flow statement.

Overview — Aurora Three Statement Forecast Model
The Brief

The goal was to build a robust, presentation-ready three statement financial model for a fictional outdoor retailer operating across both physical stores and an online channel — a business profile that reflects real complexity in retail finance.

The model needed to be genuinely dynamic: changing a single assumption such as inventory days or COGS margin should automatically flow through all three statements and leave the balance sheet balanced. It also needed to communicate clearly to a non-technical audience while holding up to scrutiny from a finance professional.

The Approach

The model is split across two sheets: an Assumptions tab where every driver lives, and a Three Statement Model tab where all outputs are calculated. This separation is deliberate — it means anyone can adjust the forecast simply by changing numbers in one place, without touching a single formula.

Industry colour convention: Blue text means a hardcoded input, black means a formula and green means a cross-sheet link. Any finance professional will immediately understand which cells are assumptions and which are derived — a small detail that signals craft.

Working capital items including accounts receivable, inventory and accounts payable are driven by days-based assumptions rather than flat percentages, which is how a CFO would actually think about them. Inventory days are set slightly higher than the historical average at 80 to 85 days to reflect the seasonal stocking patterns common in outdoor retail — buying winter product in late summer before it sells through.

The three statements are fully linked: net profit rolls into retained earnings on the balance sheet; the cash flow statement reconciles to the balance sheet cash line; depreciation runs through a PP&E schedule before feeding both the income statement and cash flow. The balance sheet check row confirms Assets = Liabilities + Equity across all ten years, reading "OK" on every column.

Key Features

Revenue split into store and online channels with separate mix assumptions. As e-commerce share grows from 32% to 42% across the forecast, the model reflects the resulting shift in fulfilment costs.

Operating expenses structured as store labour, fulfilment and logistics, marketing and promotions, rent and occupancy, and G&A — matching how a retail P&L is actually presented rather than a generic corporate template.

Working capital driven by days-based assumptions for accounts receivable, inventory and accounts payable. Inventory is set to 80 to 85 days versus the historical 73 to reflect forward-buying patterns for seasonal outdoor product.

Three dedicated roll-forward schedules for PP&E and depreciation, debt and working capital, sitting beneath the main statements to ensure every balance sheet movement is explainable rather than just arithmetically correct.

Fully linked statements throughout: net profit flows into retained earnings, depreciation flows from the PP&E schedule through the P&L and back into operating cash flow, and closing cash feeds directly to the balance sheet.

Balance sheet integrity check confirming Assets = Liabilities + Equity to within £1 across all ten years, displaying "OK" in every column — not because the numbers were forced, but because the model mechanics are correct.

The Outcome

Over the five-year forecast horizon, revenue grows from $151M in FY25 to $234M in FY30 at an 8 to 10% annual rate consistent with a maturing multi-channel retailer gaining online share. Gross margin holds firm at 63 to 64% throughout, supported by a stable COGS assumption and an improving product mix as higher-margin categories grow. The EBIT margin expands materially from 25% at the end of the historical period to nearly 33% by FY30, driven by operating leverage as the fixed rent base is spread over a larger revenue base.

Revenue growth FY21 to FY30 ($000s) — historical in dark green, forecast in bright green

The cash position grows to $327M by FY30, reflecting strong operating cash conversion as depreciation and amortisation adds back non-cash charges and working capital investment remains disciplined. The one planned debt repayment of $10M in FY29 reduces interest expense in the final year, giving a small further boost to net margin.

The model demonstrates that a well-structured financial model is more than arithmetic — it is a tool for strategic thinking. Changing the online channel mix assumption, for example, immediately shows how the shift in fulfilment costs affects EBIT margin. That is the value of keeping assumptions separated from outputs.
